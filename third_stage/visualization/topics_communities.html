Topic similarity graph for Chilean News Outlets in Twitter


What is being calculated? Toggle details »

Each media outlet is described by the frequency of use of every word in the content it emits.

Two outlets can then be considered to be similar or not based on these descriptions.

Finally, a graph can be constructed by establishing an edge between two outlets if their similarity is high enough.

A time series for a given outlet starts as the frequency of tweets containing any of the selected terms for each day. These time series are normalized by dividing each frequency by the previous non-zero one. Correlations are then calculated for each pair of time series.

What do colors/sizes mean? Toggle details »

  • Edge color is used to represent similarity values. A grey edge represents a low similarity; a red edge represents high similarity.
  • Hovering over a node displays the outlet name; hovering over an edge shows the similarity between the outlets it connects.
  • A bigger node size stands for a bigger number of followers.
  • The most connected node is always black (unless there are no connections). Nodes with no connections are always white. A linear scale between these two is used to fill the rest of the nodes.
